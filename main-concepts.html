Main Concepts

Understanding key concepts related to fake news is essential for navigating the modern media landscape. Let's explore some main concepts:

  1. Media Literacy: Media literacy is the ability to access, analyze, evaluate, and create media in various forms. It involves critical thinking skills and an understanding of how media messages are constructed and disseminated.
  2. Fact-Checking: Fact-checking is the process of verifying the accuracy of information presented as factual. It involves examining sources, corroborating evidence, and assessing the credibility of claims.
  3. Confirmation Bias: Confirmation bias is the tendency to search for, interpret, and favor information that confirms one's preexisting beliefs or hypotheses while disregarding contradictory evidence.
  4. Filter Bubbles: Filter bubbles are personalized information ecosystems created by algorithms that selectively present content based on a user's past behavior and preferences.
